Instructions

You have been contacted via the Amazon MTurk platform to invite you to participate in our study investigating the effect of user comments on the trustworthiness of Facebook posts.
To successfully complete the HIT, you will be required to complete TWO tasks.
Please attempt this survey on a laptop or a desktop computer.

Tasks

Task 1: You will be required to analyse 28 recent Facebook posts, each containing a news article. You will be asked to provide your opinion on the trustworthiness of each post, and rate how likely you are to like, comment, fact check, share (to your wall) or report it, both before and after seeing user comments (STRICTLY WITHOUT browsing the Internet). This process will be repeated 28 times, and is estimated to take up 40 minutes.

Task 2: Upon successful completion of Task 1, you will be required to complete a 44-item personality questionnaire. This is expected to take 2-3 minutes to complete.

** You are expected to complete BOTH tasks in one sitting, which will take a maximum of 45 minutes.
** Do NOT refresh the page, or use forward and back controls of the web browser during the experiment. If you do so, your data will be lost and you will not receive any payment.

Other Information

No identifying information will be collected as part of our research data, and while participation will involve the recording of your worker ID, this will be removed from the data immediately after download. We will therefore not be able to identify your particular responses in our data. We do not anticipate any risks to you from participation. You will receive the agreed upon value of $10 (USD) for the completion of the HIT. Participation is voluntary and you have the right to withdraw from further participation at any stage up to the submission of your data. However, you will not receive any payment if you decide to abandon the task before completion. Submission of the final results in Task 2 will be taken as an indication of your consent.

If you would like more information about the project, please contact the researchers using the Amazon's communication functionality.

Task 1
You will see screenshots of several Facebook posts.
Imagine that they appear on your news feed as shared by a distant friend.
Read the posts carefully and,
Indicate your familiarity with the Facebook post.
Rate the trustworthiness of the post, and provide your confidence on the rating.
Indicate how likely you are to like, share, comment, fact check or report the post.
See how other Facebook users have commented on the post.
After reading their comments, identify how others feel about the trustworthiness of the post.
